Experimental Method and Systematic Effects

Cs and Fr fountains allow the measurements to be done in free space and in free fall.

  • No collisions with gasses or walls.
  • No confining lasers or A.C. Stark shifts.
  • No applied static or time-varying magnetic fields.
  • Optical state preparation and analysis in a region free of applied magnetic and electric fields.
  • Only a static electric field between state preparation and analysis.


Verifying Sensitivity

We plan to verify sensitivity using three sets of tests involving both electric and magnetic fields. We will measure the quadratic Stark shift [1] for a very small electric field and the change in quadratic Stark shift for a small change in electric field. The shifts involved will be comparable to the linear Stark shift we would expect from an EDM at our claimed level of sensitivity. We will measure the Zeeman effect [2] for a very small applied magnetic field which we will also reverse. And we will introduce a motional systematic effect to produce a very small shift that is linear in a large applied electric field.

Systematic Effect Details

A table of contents for the Project Document's systematic effects chapters is shown below. Chapter 5 on Motional Systematic Effects can be downloaded here, Chapter 6 on Other Systematic Effects, downloaded here, the Appendicies A - D downloaded here, and the Bibliography for the entire report downloaded here. The calculations in Chapter 5 have been expanded and published[1]

The experiments use a strong electric field to set the axis of quantization and lift the degeneracy between states of different M2, where M is the magnetic quantum number of the state, allowing us to perform the experiments in zero magnetic field. We prepare a superpositions of different +M and -M states, whose composition can be changed by rotating the laser polarization. As described in Ref. [1] and Chapter 5, different ±M states have different sensitivities to motional systematic effects and to an electron EDM. By changing the laser polarization, we can change the sensitivity to an EDM and to motional systematic effects (including making the EDM sensitivity zero).

And because we use a superposition of +M and -M states, an incomplete reversal of the electric field does not lead to a systematic effect. The tensor polarizability scales with M as M2 and with electric field E as E2, so that absent an EDM, +M and -M states have the same interaction with the electric field.

We also adjust the electric field so that the phase advance due to the Stark effect is, for every M state a multiple of π and have demonstrated its use in a proof-of-principle cesium fountain EDM experiment[2].
