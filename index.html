New Electron Electric Dipole Moment Experiments

Using Ultra-Cold Cesium and Francium Atom Fountains


Summary


  • The experimental upper limit to the electron electric dipole moment (EDM) is consequential because it places constraints on models of Dark Matter, on models of Baryon Asymmetry, Axion-like particles, Supersymmetry, and other beyond Standard Model physics. It places constraints on muon g-2 experiments and future colliders.

  • This was not previously the case when the electron EDM limit was large and only a false positive was of concern.
    To avoid wrong results, electron EDM experiments must:
    • be based on a rigorous theoretical foundation,
    • be based on a complete understanding of the experimental system, and
    • must include tests to verify the experimental sensitivity to an EDM and to EDM-mimicking effects.
    Our experiments using ultra-cold cesium and francium fountains will meet all of these requirements.
