  • Other Work

    • Almost all of us have worked with accelerators; either as experimenters or designers, and or building, operating, and managing accelerator facilities. Group members have performed measurements at Brookhaven AGS (ultra-relativistic atomic collisions with Au); CERN SPS (ultra-relativistic atomic collisions); Fermilab Antiproton Accumulator (antihydrogen); GANIL (electron-photons scattering); LBNL Advanced Light Source (photodissociation of hydrogen, molecular fragmentation); LBNL Bevalac (Lamb Shift in few electron Uranium, and bound-free pair production in relativistic atomic collisions); , and SLAC (Z decay). There are many publications in the literature from our work.

    *There is a second Harvey Gould who does not do experiments.

  • Working with Us


    • Who May be Interested

      • Our small, but difficult experiments may appeal to people interested in fundamental physics and in doing hands-on precision measurements, acquiring new experimental skills, and working with LBNL staff to solve a wide range of unique problems. Much of the technical knowledge needed is acquired on the job.

      If you are interested in working on an electron EDM experiment but would prefer to compete with us, we can help you get started. Discovery of an electron EDM will likely require an independent confirming experiment (think dark energy).


    • PostDoctoral Fellow

      • We will be posting a position for an LBNL PostDoctoral Fellow in the Accelerator Technology and Applied Physics (ATAP) Division to be followed by additional positions. ATAP Division is part of the LBNL Physical Sciences Area and its pay scale and culture is aligned with that of Physics and Nuclear Science Divisions. In addition to the incentives, career opportunities, and benefits listed here, and benefits specific to PostDocs, LBNL PostDocs are represented by the International Union, United Automobile, Aerospace and Agricultural Implement Workers of America, AFL-CIO and its local Union 5810.


    • Collaborations

      • Through our accelerator work, we have a long history of collaboration with other groups and individuals at all professional levels. Past and present collaborators include retirees, LBNL staff, university faculty, visiting faculty, post doctoral fellows, high school Physics teachers, graduate student research assistants, post bacalaureate fellows, undergraduate assistants, and unpaid spouses.

      Our work would not be possible without the work of a large number of talented, knowledgable, and hard-working theoreists. We look forward to continuing this tradition.

      Limited-term paid positions, travel support, visits, unpaid positions, and other arrangements, can be made available through the Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.

      Contact Harvey Gould if you are interested in working with us at LBNL on the Cs EDM experiment or magnetic shielding demagnetization, or at TRIUMF on the Fr EDM experiment or on a theory question related to this work.

      For work on francium, we are collaborating with scientists at TRIUMF and anticipate widening the collaboration to meet the needs of an accelerator based experiment.
