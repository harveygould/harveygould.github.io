Magnetic Shielding Tests


Effective and stable magnetic shielding is essential because the Earth has a magnetic field and the electron has a large magnetic dipole moment. Few, if any, electron EDM experiments have tested magnetic shielding. We have been testing ours and are preparing a second publication about previously unreported properties that could affect an EDM experiment.


Magnetic Shields


The permeability of a shield can be reduced due to less than complete adherence to the post fabrication annealing protocol and by lapses in care in handling the annealed shields. To help avoid these problems and establish a reproducible procedure for future shields, we did the following: after inspecting the Carpenter HyMu “80”TM alloy (80% Ni, 4.2% Mo, balance Fe), the test shields were fabricated (cut, machined, rolled, and welded) at the LBNL main machine shop.

They were annealed in a hydrogen atmosphere by Thermo-Fusion in Hayward CA, who documented the full 1120 oC twelve-hour heating and cooling cycle. The annealed shields were picked up at Thermo-Fusion by Feinberg and Gould, wrapped in cushioning material, packaged in protective fiberboard tubes, returned to LBNL in a station wagon, and hand delivered to the laboratory where the measurements were performed. The shields out-performed similar shields described in the literature.


Magnetic Shielding Measurements

We have observed previously unreported time and temperature dependences of shield magnetization and have written two papers detailing our findings[1,2]. Our next set of tests will be on shield demagnetization as magnetic shields are themselves highly magnetic.
